Malayan Civet

Scientific name: Viverra tangalunga

Conservation Status: Least Concern

Estimated Population: unknown

Avg. Lifespan: 11-12 years

The Malayan Civet is a nocturnal, cat-like mammal native to Southeast Asia, commonly found across Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. Recognizable by its elongated body, pointed snout, and distinct dark spots and stripes, it thrives in both natural and human-modified environments. It plays an important ecological role as a seed disperser and pest controller in tropical ecosystems. Unlike other civet species, it adapts well to a range of environments, including forests, farmlands, and village outskirts.

This species is omnivorous, feeding on insects, small mammals, reptiles, fruits, eggs, and roots. It inhabits forests, secondary growth areas, cultivated land, and the outskirts of villages, showing great adaptability to changing habitats. The civet’s solitary and nocturnal nature allows it to avoid predators and human activity, though it occasionally ventures near farms or settlements.

Despite its adaptability, the Malayan Civet faces threats from habitat destruction, deforestation, and hunting. Agricultural expansion and timber harvesting reduce forest cover, while its tendency to raid poultry coops and fruit orchards often brings it into conflict with humans. In some areas, civets are legally trapped or shot as pests. Conservation efforts focus on habitat preservation and minimizing human-wildlife conflict.

Quick Facts

Names

General Name: Malayan Civet
Scientific name: Viverra tangalunga

Diet

Omnivorous: insects, snakes, frogs, fruits & small rodents

Lifespan

~10-20 years

Habitat

Forests, secondary habitats, cultivated lands, & near human settlements

Behaviour

Mostly nocturnal; active on ground but capable climber when foraging

Population

Exact numbers unknown; populations declining in disturbed & deforested areas

Threats

habitat loss, hunting, trapping & conflicts with farmers who view them as pests

Conservation Efforts

Protected in several reserves; habitat preservation, reduction of human conflict
